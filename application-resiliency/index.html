## The 12 Factors ![12 Commandments](images/commandments.gif) These help to ensure that your applications can scale to meet growing demand and can be deployed to new servers at a moments notice.
## The 12 Factors It's critical that you follow these when designing and developing **any** application. These can help to massively improve how you work with Monolithic legacy applications.
## The 12 Factors - The Highlights Some of the more useful factors are:
## The 12 Factors - II. Dependencies Explicitly declare and isolate dependencies. Technologies like Docker help you achieve this.
## The 12 Factors - III. Config Store config in the environment. CloudFoundry helps you to achieve this by injecting credentials into the applications environment at startup.
## The 12 Factors - VI. Processes Execute the app as one or more stateless processes. Store all state in some form of database. If you lose one process, it doesn't impact you, just spin up another to replace it.
## The 12 Factors - VIII. Concurrency Scale out via the process model. This is similar to VI. and allows you to front your application with a load balancer and scale to handle dynamic workload.
## The 12 Factors - XI. Logs Treat logs as event streams. Don't store logs on the file system, stream them out to log handling services such as Splunk and handle them as events.
## The 12 Factors - XI. Logs Treat logs as event streams. This greatly improves the way you debug your distributed systems.
